Info

Welcome to the Steel Dores web application!

The purpose of this app is to allow users to practice playing the steel pans. Steel pans are percussion instruments from Trinidad and Tobago made from 55-gallon industrial drums. They are chromatically-pitched; each indent in a steel pan corresponds to a different pitch. There are several different types of steel drums. When played together in a band, they create a unique sound that cannot be emulated by other instruments.

Steel Dores allows user to practice making music on virtual steel pans. We provide six types of pans for users to choose from: lead, double tenor, double second, guitar/cello, tenor bass, and six bass. To use the app, choose a pan type from our Pan Selection page. Then, tap or click on the pan's indents to hear the many notes that the pan can produce.

This application was created specifically for the steel band program at Vanderbilt University's Blair School of Music in Nashville, Tennessee. For more information on the steel band program, click here.
